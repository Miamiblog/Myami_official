The Foundation Priority

Move MYAMI From Your Head Into the World

Phase 1 has one primary goal: make MYAMI real. Not real in documents — real in the world. A live website. Active social accounts. Real clients. Real revenue. Real work in a portfolio that proves every claim the brand makes about itself.

Everything built so far — the brand identity, the website, the manifesto, the brand bible — is the infrastructure that makes Phase 1 possible. Now the work is execution. Consistent, relentless, public execution.

"Phase 1 is not about scale. It is about proof. Proof that MYAMI can deliver. Proof that clients will pay. Proof that the work is as good as the brand promises it will be."

The Retainer Priority

Project work pays once. Retainers pay every month. The Phase 1 revenue goal is to convert at least 2–3 clients onto monthly retainers by Month 6. A base of $1,500–3,000 in recurring monthly revenue is more valuable than any single large project.

The Upsell Ladder

Every Tech client is a Media prospect. Every Media client is an Entertainment prospect. Every client who comes in through any door should be walking up the MYAMI ladder — from a single service to a full brand partnership. The goal is lifetime client value, not single-transaction revenue.

Decision Framework
How MYAMI makes decisions at every stage of growth.
The Framework

Four Questions. Every Decision.

Every significant decision MYAMI makes — a new client, a new service, a hire, a partnership, a product — gets run through these four questions. If it can't pass all four, the answer is no.

Q1
Does this make us look like we've already arrived? Every touchpoint is brand. If a decision makes MYAMI look like it's trying too hard, compromising quality, or chasing revenue at the expense of reputation — the answer is no.
Q2
Does this serve the phase we're in? Phase 1 needs clients and cash. Phase 2 needs scale and team. Phase 3 needs brand and systems. An opportunity that's right for Phase 3 but wrong for Phase 1 is still wrong for Phase 1.
Q3
Can we deliver this excellently? MYAMI will not take work it cannot do at the level the brand promises. Average delivery is worse than no delivery — it leaves a client with evidence that the brand is wrong about itself.
Q4
Does this move something forward? Revenue, reputation, relationships, or skills. If a decision doesn't clearly advance at least one of these four, it is a distraction. MYAMI's most limited resource in Phase 1 is time — spend it on things that compound.
Non-Negotiables

The Rules That Never Change

These apply in Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, and every phase after. They are not guidelines — they are the floor below which MYAMI does not go, regardless of circumstances or pressure.

01
The work comes first. MYAMI's brand exists to serve what it builds — not the other way around. When the brand and the work are in conflict, the work wins. Always.
02
Deliver what you promise. Underpromise and overdeliver. Never the reverse. A client who gets more than they expected becomes a referral. A client who gets less becomes a warning.
03
Be selective. MYAMI does not take every project. It takes the ones where it can do something worth doing. Saying no to the wrong clients protects the capacity to say yes to the right ones.
04
Ship consistently. Every week there is a deliverable. A piece of content, a client project, a new capability built. Consistency compounds. Silence does not.
05
Document everything. Every project, every lesson, every result. The portfolio is not just a sales tool — it is the evidence that MYAMI is what it says it is. Build it deliberately from day one.
06
Never compete on price. MYAMI competes on quality, conviction, and cultural credibility. The moment MYAMI starts positioning on price, it stops being a premium brand. Price communicates value — protect it.
07
Bias toward action. Analysis is useful. Planning is useful. But MYAMI moves — it builds, ships, and learns from real output. The plan serves the execution, not the other way around.
